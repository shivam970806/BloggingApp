Staying informed within our respective niche industries and personal interests would require a dozen monthly magazine subscriptions in the past. With the plethora of online blogs hosting information on any subject you can imagine, the overload of info needs to be narrowed down based on the central bloggers that match your needs. Knowing in advance that your blog is not going to be the first or last digital news hub for your subject of interest means that you better have a name that grabs like-minded individuals in a hurry. Let’s take a moment to see what we can learn from long-lasting successful blogs that have cut through the competition.

If you’re still stuck on creating some name ideas, here’s a breakdown of some of the top brands and businesses in the industry. TechCrunch

Michael Arrington, a blogger with a background as a mergers and acquisitions lawyer handling tech deals in California, started TechCrunch by covering some of the best up-and-coming tech companies and entrepreneurs from all over the planet. How big did TechCrunch get? It was acquired by AOL for $30 Million dollars. Taking technology and tacking on the action word “crunch” lets you know that the blog is taking a bite out of the tech industry and is breaking it down into a consumable format for readers. It is also vivid enough to remember because it uses words understood by those of use outside of the tech space. 

The Daily Dish

Author, editor, and blogger Andrew Sullivan who has written for TIME, The Atlantic, and The Daily Beast, began The Daily Dish in 2000, turning it into one of the biggest political blogs that evolved into a subscription-based model. Whether you look at The Daily Dish and think about food nutrition or dishing out dirt in a gossip sense, it paints a picture bigger than politics. I also make this formal subject sounds way more entertaining. 

Smart Passive Income

Entrepreneur and blogger Pat Flynn started “SmartPassiveIncome.com after losing his job and needing to support his family. Through disclosing his monthly earnings with complete transparency, he has built an expansive following, inspiring people to earn online incomes. Is your blog niche one specific enough that it would benefit from telling people literally what it is all about, like Smart Passive Income? There is such a thing as being too abstract and confusing people. 

Four Hour Work Week

Tim Ferriss, the self-proclaimed life hacker and author of the bestseller 4 Hour Work Week, blogs about the idea of “lifestyle design,” showing people how to work from anywhere in the world on their laptops. Who wouldn’t want to reduce their weekly workload to four hours? What goal or promise can your blog name offer your audience?
